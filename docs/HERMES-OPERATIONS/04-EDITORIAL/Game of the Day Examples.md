**Version:** 1.0
**Last Modified:** 2025-06-06

## Purpose

Reference examples of well-written Game of the Day content and annotation of what makes them effective.

---

## Example 1 — Division Race Context

> **Cubs at Cardinals | 7:15 PM ET | Busch Stadium**
>
> *With St. Louis one game back in the NL Central, this three-game series opening carries genuine weight. The Cardinals have won seven of ten and are playing the kind of consistent baseball they need to sustain a race, while Chicago enters having dropped four of its last six after a strong April.*
>
> *On the mound: Marcus Stroman (4–3, 3.12 ERA) faces Miles Mikolas (3–4, 4.01 ERA). Stroman has been sharp over his last four starts — 26.1 innings, 6 earned runs — and has historically performed well in road environments. Mikolas has been serviceable but inconsistent, allowing three or more runs in four of his last seven outings.*
>
> *Key factor: Chicago's left-handed bats — Dansby Swanson, Ian Happ — have struggled against right-handed sinker-heavy pitchers this season, posting a combined .211 average. Mikolas's sinker usage will be worth watching early.*

**What works:**
- Identification line is clean: teams, time, venue.
- "Why it matters" section is specific — one game back, recent records given.
- Pitching section goes beyond season ERA to recent form (last 4 starts).
- Key factor is genuinely specific: names the hitters, names the matchup type, cites the stat.

## Example 2 — Pitching Matchup Quality

> **Phillies at Braves | 7:20 PM ET | Truist Park**
>
> *Neither team is in a must-win situation, but this matchup earns Game of the Day on the strength of the pitching alone: Zack Wheeler vs. Spencer Strider, two of the better starters in the NL, meeting in a venue that tends to produce low-scoring outcomes.*
>
> *Wheeler enters at 2.44 ERA on the season, with 11 strikeouts per nine across his last five starts. Strider has been equally dominant — 2.61 ERA, 13 K/9 — though he missed a start earlier in the month with a blister and this will be his second outing since returning.*
>
> *Key factor: The blister history matters here. Strider's pitch counts have been managed conservatively since returning. If Atlanta's bullpen is needed in the sixth or seventh, Philadelphia's lineup — ranked fifth in OPS vs. relievers this season — becomes the operative variable.*

**What works:**
- Honest about why it's the pick: the pitching matchup, not the standings situation.
- Strider's recent health history is noted factually and connected to a concrete game impact.
- Key factor directly flows from a specific, data-supported risk.

## Common Errors to Avoid

**Missing the "why":**
> "Dodgers at Giants is always a good matchup."

Rivalry is not a reason. What is actually at stake today?

**Prediction disguised as analysis:**
> "Expect this one to go deep into extras — both bullpens have been lights out."

"Expect" is a prediction. Remove it. State what the data shows about the bullpens and let the reader draw the conclusion.

**Generic key factor:**
> "Home field advantage could be significant tonight."

Home field advantage exists every night. The key factor section should identify something specific to this game, this week, these players.
