# Press Box Examples
**Version:** 1.0
**Last Modified:** 2025-06-06

---

## Purpose

This document provides reference examples of well-written Press Box content and annotated notes on what makes each example effective. Use these as calibration when evaluating generated output.

---

## Example 1 — Strong Lead Item

> **The Dodgers ended their five-game skid with a 7–2 win over the Padres on Tuesday, getting seven strong innings from Tyler Glasnow and a three-run shot from Freddie Freeman in the fifth. Los Angeles had dropped four straight at home heading into the series and entered the night three games back in the NL West.**
>
> *Cincinnati quietly moved into a wild card position after sweeping a home series against Pittsburgh. The Reds are now 8–3 in their last 11 and have allowed fewer than three runs in six of those games.*
>
> *Aaron Judge reached 20 home runs faster than any Yankee since Mickey Mantle, connecting twice in a 9–3 blowout over Baltimore. New York has won seven of its last nine.*
>
> *Tonight's most consequential game: Atlanta visits Philadelphia with first place in the NL East at stake. Both clubs enter at 41–28.*

**What works:**
- Lead item opens with the result, then the context (5-game skid, 3 back in division). Gives the reader both what happened and why it matters.
- Each item in the body is a self-contained observation — not a list of facts, but a complete thought.
- The forward-looking item is specific: names the teams, states the stakes, gives the records.
- No filler. No manufactured drama. Every sentence earns its place.

## Example 2 — Off Day / Minimal Games

> **With just four games on the schedule Monday, the most significant action came in the AL Central, where Minnesota held off Chicago 4–3 in 10 innings to move into a half-game lead over Cleveland. The Twins have won five of six and are playing their best baseball since April.**
>
> *San Francisco's rotation depth took another hit when Logan Webb was scratched from Tuesday's start with forearm tightness. The Giants have not announced a timeline.*
>
> *Tuesday brings a light schedule as well — eight games — but the highlight matchup is Houston visiting Seattle in what amounts to an early-season statement game for two AL West contenders.*

**What works:**
- Doesn't pretend a slow Monday was more interesting than it was. Leads with the most meaningful game available.
- Injury note is factual and appropriately restrained — "no timeline announced" rather than projecting severity.
- Forward-looking item acknowledges the light schedule honestly.

## Common Errors to Avoid

**Too vague:**
> "It was a big night across the league, with several teams making statements in important games."

No facts. No names. Nothing to read here.

**Over-inflated:**
> "In what may be a turning point in the NL East race, the Braves delivered a crushing blow..."

It's June. Nothing in June is a turning point unless the data actually supports that framing.

**Burying the lead:**
> "After a slow start and some defensive miscues in the early innings, the Cubs eventually rallied to..."

Start with what happened, not how you got there.
