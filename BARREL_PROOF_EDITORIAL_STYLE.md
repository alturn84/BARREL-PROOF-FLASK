# Barrel Proof Baseball — Editorial Style Guide

*This guide governs all Hermes-generated content. Update it as standards evolve.*

---

## Voice

Barrel Proof reads like a baseball newspaper from the 1940s — authoritative,
specific, economical. Think Red Smith, not ESPN.com. Think wire brief, not
blog post. Every sentence should earn its place.

---

## Headlines

- Active voice
- Player names when the player is the story
- Team nicknames when the team is the story (Yankees, not New York)
- 6 to 12 words
- No clickbait, no hyperbole
- No punctuation at the end unless it's a deliberate stylistic choice
- Do not include the score

**Good:**
- "Soderstrom Powers Oakland Past Cubs in Ten Innings"
- "Skubal Strikes Out Nine as Tigers Rout Tampa Bay"
- "Alonso Homer in Seventh Lifts Mets Past Seattle"

**Bad:**
- "New York beats Seattle"
- "An incredible comeback in extras"
- "ATH wins it in the 10th"

---

## Game Summaries

- 1–2 sentences, 35–60 words
- Use team nicknames, not city names
- Start with the key moment or player, not the winning team
- Name one specific player with a specific number or play
- Explain why the game turned
- Mention the score once

**Good:**
"Tarik Skubal struck out nine and Detroit backed him with three home runs in a convincing win over Tampa Bay."
"Pete Alonso's seventh-inning homer broke a 2-2 tie as the Mets held off Seattle, 4-2."

**Bad:**
"New York beat Seattle 4-2. The winning pitcher allowed 2 earned runs."
"The decisive blow came in the seventh inning."

---

## Game of the Day

- 3 paragraphs, 175–300 words
- Paragraph 1: Result and decisive moment — start with action
- Paragraph 2: How the game turned — one inning, one sequence
- Paragraph 3: Key performer and standings context
- Use full team names on first reference
- Name the winning and losing pitcher
- Name the key offensive performer with specific numbers
- Do not repeat the score more than once

---

## Press Box Notebook

- 6 to 10 bullets
- One sentence per bullet, max 25 words
- Cover only: injuries, IL moves, DFA, trades, signings, call-ups,
  rehab assignments, milestones, suspensions
- No game recaps, no scores, no standings
- Priority order: injuries first, then DFA, trades, signings, call-ups

---

## Game to Watch

- Explain why the reader should watch — be specific
- Name the pitchers if they are the reason
- Reference standings if they are the reason
- Reference the rivalry if it exists
- Never mention: composite score, algorithm, ranking, model, slate score

**Good:**
"Zack Wheeler and Lucas Giolito headline one of Thursday's stronger pitching matchups."
"Both teams are within two games of first — this one has real standings weight."

**Bad:**
"Best composite score on today's slate."
"This is the strongest overall matchup on today's schedule."

---

## Banned Phrases

Never use these in any generated content:

- neither side would yield
- the contest
- the decisive blow
- the outcome underscores
- hopes were dashed
- the affair
- ultimately prevailed
- the game unfolded
- the club
- the slate
- best composite score
- proved the difference
- strong performance
- great outing
- allowed X earned runs
- the story is full
- the bullpens are empty
- in a game that
- fundamentally shifting
